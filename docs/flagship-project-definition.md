# Flagship Project Definition

## Selected First-Cycle Format

Hope Sessions: New Work Recording Project

## Why This Format Wins

This is the strongest first-cycle shape because it is narrow, defensible, artist-centered, and directly aligned with the ASC Artist Support criteria. It prioritizes artistic development over broad program promises and avoids the appearance of asking a funder to subsidize the studio as a business.

It also makes practical sense for Paul Andrejack's situation. Andrejack's album Hope already establishes the artistic theme. The next step should not be an overbuilt community program. It should be a clearly scoped new recording project that extends the themes, sound, and creative ambition of Hope into a new body of work.

## Project Summary

Paul Andrejack will develop, record, and complete a focused cycle of new music under the Hope Sessions banner. The first-cycle version should be defined as a three-song recording and release-readiness project that advances the artistic world introduced by Andrejack's album Hope.

The project uses PMA Recording Studios as the working environment and artistic infrastructure, but the proposal itself is centered on the music, the creative process, and the applicant's artistic growth. The strongest funding logic is that the project supports the creation and finishing of new work, strengthens Andrejack's ability to bring that work to audiences, and creates a documented artistic bridge from prior work to the next chapter.

## Core Outputs

1. Three fully developed and professionally finished songs.
2. Final recordings prepared for release readiness.
3. A defined work-sample package and project documentation set that can support future grant and partner opportunities.
4. A clearer artistic platform for future Hope Sessions access, workshop, or story-based community formats.

## What The Grant Should Support

The cleanest ask is for project costs directly tied to artistic development and completion of the new work.

Strong categories include:

- outside musician or collaborator fees
- specialist production support if needed
- mixing or mastering support
- project materials and session-specific costs
- limited promotional or release-readiness materials if permitted by local guidelines

## What The Grant Should Not Center

Avoid building the project around:

- general studio overhead
- business expansion
- capital improvements framed as business upgrades
- vague community programming promises that the first cycle cannot yet deliver

## Artistic Development Case

This project should be framed as the next serious step in Paul Andrejack's creative practice. The argument is not just that he wants to record more music. The argument is that this project allows him to deepen composition, arrangement, recording, and finishing discipline while developing a more cohesive and publicly resonant artistic identity around Hope.

## Public-Benefit Positioning

For the first-cycle ASC application, public benefit should be present but disciplined. The primary case is artistic advancement. The secondary case is that the project lays the groundwork for a larger Hope Sessions model that can later expand access for other artists through future collaborations, partner activity, and community-facing programming.

## Why Three Songs

Three songs is the best current assumption because it is ambitious enough to feel real and small enough to remain feasible under a grant in the $500 to $3,000 range. It creates enough material to show artistic range and momentum without forcing the budget into an inflated or fragile shape.

## Recommended Working Timeline

1. Pre-production and final songwriting
2. Tracking and arrangement refinement
3. Overdubs and editing
4. Mixing and mastering
5. Release-readiness and documentation assembly

## Recommended Short Description

Hope Sessions: New Work Recording Project is a three-song recording and finishing cycle led by Paul Andrejack in Denver, North Carolina. Building from the artistic foundation of Andrejack's album Hope, the project supports the creation of new original music that advances the next chapter of his creative practice while laying the groundwork for a broader artist-development and access initiative.
