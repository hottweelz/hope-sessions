# Hope Sessions Page Build Brief

## Build Goal

Implement a modern one-page HTML5/CSS experience for Hope Sessions that can live on the PMA domain as a supplemental initiative page.

## Recommended URL Strategy

Preferred:

- existing PMA domain with a dedicated Hope page or subdirectory

Examples:

- /hope-sessions/
- /initiatives/hope-sessions/

This keeps existing PMA authority, avoids domain sprawl, and makes the page easy to reference in grant and outreach conversations.

## Content Inputs

Use these source docs:

- narrative bank
- Hope initiative concept
- Hope page copy
- asset and proof checklist

## Component List

1. sticky header with anchor navigation
2. hero with dual CTA
3. editorial story section
4. founder credibility section
5. initiative/program section
6. proof/media section
7. partner pathway section
8. artist pathway section
9. contact form or CTA block
10. footer with PMA and social links

## Form Requirements

Minimum fields:

- full name
- email
- organization or role
- inquiry type
- message

Suggested inquiry types:

- partner inquiry
- artist inquiry
- media inquiry
- studio inquiry
- general inquiry

## Visual Direction

The design should feel contemporary and editorial, not corporate and not generic nonprofit. It should be visually grounded in music, recording, and atmosphere.

Suggested direction:

- strong typographic hero
- warm but high-contrast palette
- textured or atmospheric background treatment
- documentary-style images over stock-style polish
- clear rhythm between dense narrative and open visual breathing room

## Required Proof Blocks Before Design Lock

1. approved founder bio
2. at least two safe-to-publish work samples or embeds
3. at least one strong studio or artist image
4. at least one publicly safe credibility statement, quote, or named proof point

## Technical Expectations

1. semantic HTML5 sections
2. CSS variables for theme control
3. responsive layout from mobile upward
4. accessible form labels and focus states
5. reduced-motion support
6. image optimization and lazy loading where appropriate
7. no heavy framework required for first release unless the host stack already dictates one

## QA Requirements

1. mobile and desktop verification
2. keyboard-only navigation
3. form submission and error-state testing
4. basic performance review
5. content proof review by Reality Checker and Evidence Collector roles before launch

## Launch Constraint

Do not launch the page with placeholder proof. If the visual shell is ready before the evidence is ready, hold launch until the proof section is credible.
