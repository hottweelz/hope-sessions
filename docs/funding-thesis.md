# Funding Thesis

## One-Page Thesis

The most fundable version of this project is not a request to subsidize a recording business. The fundable version is an artist-centered and community-facing creative program in which the studio functions as infrastructure for artistic production, artist access, education, preservation, and community benefit.

For this workspace, the most practical immediate path is the North Carolina Artist Support Grants route. The North Carolina Arts Council states that Artist Support Grants are administered through regional consortia of local arts councils and are open to professional artists pursuing projects that advance their artistic and professional development. Lincoln County is listed in the consortium administered by Arts & Science Council Charlotte-Mecklenburg, which makes that consortium the current verified gateway to the strongest direct individual opportunity for a Denver, North Carolina applicant.

That matters because the other attractive statewide routes are structurally different. Grassroots Arts Program funding is county-partner and organization based, not a direct individual-artist path. Project Support Grants are designed for nonprofit organizations with a two-year track record and at least $20,000 in prior-year cash operating expenses, though a fiscal agent can sometimes be used. Those routes become relevant only after there is a defined public-benefit program and either a qualified partner or a fiscal-agent relationship.

The recommended wrapper for the first application cycle is a Hope-centered version of Emerging Artist Recording Sessions. Paul Andrejack and the band Andrejack already have an authentic artistic anchor in the album Hope, which creates a stronger narrative spine than a generic studio-access proposal. That matters because it gives the initiative a clear thematic center: music as a vehicle for resilience, creative possibility, and community-rooted encouragement. The wrapper is still fundable under the same doctrine as before because the studio remains infrastructure rather than the mission itself. In the immediate cycle, Hope should function as the artistic foundation for a defined recording project and a public-benefit story about creation, access, and artist development. In later cycles, the same theme can expand into workshops, reduced-cost sessions, storytelling-based collaborations, or preservation work without losing coherence.

The strategic sequence is straightforward. First, pursue the direct artist pathway through Artist Support Grants with a tightly scoped Hope-centered project that advances Paul Andrejack's artistic practice and uses Andrejack's prior work as proof of seriousness and continuity. Second, build the reusable materials that every future application will need: narrative bank, budget template, evidence kit, and submission tracker. Third, use that package to open partnership conversations with nonprofits, schools, churches, and arts organizations around Hope Sessions, workshops, artist-access days, or preservation activity. Those partnerships are the bridge to larger pass-through, organization-based, or fiscal-agent-supported opportunities.

The current posture is GO for discovery and preparation, but NO-GO for submission until the remaining evidence gaps are closed. The highest-value next actions are to confirm the current ASC timeline for the 2026 cycle, define the exact Hope initiative flagship project, assemble work samples including material connected to Hope, and test the Hope-centered wrapper with at least three likely partners.

## Fit-Ranked Targets

1. North Carolina Artist Support Grants via Arts & Science Council Charlotte-Mecklenburg for Lincoln County.
2. Local or regional arts-council pass-through opportunities relevant to Lincoln County.
3. Community foundation or local artist-development grants in the broader Denver, Lincoln County, or Charlotte region.
4. Partner-led budgets with nonprofits, schools, churches, and arts organizations.
5. Education or workforce-development mini-grants tied to recording, production, and youth programming.
6. Grassroots or community arts opportunities once a qualified partner is attached.
7. Cultural preservation and documentation grants once a preservation-specific project is defined.
8. National artist fellowships and private grantmakers after the base package is stronger.
9. Project Support or federal pass-through opportunities only if a qualifying fiscal agent or nonprofit partner is secured.

## Missing Information

1. Current applicant legal posture: individual artist, business entity, or both.
2. Current residency-proof documents available for applications.
3. Final flagship Hope project scope for the first submission.
4. Available work samples, bio, resume, and prior releases, especially materials tied to Andrejack and Hope.
5. Budget inputs for artist fees, engineering time, musician fees, outreach, and participant support.
6. Whether there are existing community partners or letters of support in reach.
7. Whether the applicant wants the first ask framed as creation only, Hope Sessions access work, workshops, or preservation.
8. Confirmation from ASC on the active 2026 local deadline and local application materials.

## Decision Standard

An opportunity is submission-ready only when geography, entity type, residency, and any fiscal-agent requirement are verified; the project is framed around artistic or community outcomes rather than business subsidy; the budget matches the narrative; and the required attachments are complete.
