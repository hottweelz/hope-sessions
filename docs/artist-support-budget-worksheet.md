# Artist Support Budget Worksheet

## Purpose

This worksheet turns the Hope Sessions three-song project into a grant-ready budget scaffold without inventing final costs. Replace the draft assumptions with actual quotes or real working estimates before submission.

## Project Budget Strategy

The budget should stay narrow and directly tied to completion of the three-song cycle. PMA Recording Studios should be treated as in-kind infrastructure rather than as the primary expense line.

## Working Budget Cap

- Working grant target: up to $3,000
- Current planning assumption: build a version that can scale down if the final ask needs to be lower

## Budget Build Rules

1. Every line item must connect to the three-song project narrative.
2. Avoid broad overhead language.
3. Do not make equipment the emotional center of the application.
4. If artist fee is used, describe it as project labor for writing, recording, and completion.
5. If collaborator costs are used, show why they improve feasibility or artistic quality.

## Budget Narrative Prompts

- Which expenses are essential for the songs to reach finished quality?
- Which costs are project-specific and not just normal studio operations?
- Which lines best show artistic advancement rather than business maintenance?
- Which costs can be covered in-kind so the grant request stays clean and focused?

## Decision Point

Before finalizing the budget, decide whether the strongest ask is:

1. artist fee plus finishing support
2. collaborator support plus finishing support
3. a balanced mix of artist fee, collaborators, and finishing support
