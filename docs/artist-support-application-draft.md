Where facts are still unverified or applicant-specific, placeholders are marked clearly and should be finalized before submission.

## Application Title

Hope Sessions: New Work Recording Project

## Applicant Name

Paul Andrejack

## One-Sentence Project Summary

Paul Andrejack will create and complete a focused three-song recording project under the Hope Sessions banner, building from the artistic foundation of Andrejack's album Hope to advance his songwriting, production, and release-ready body of work.

## Short Applicant Bio

Paul Andrejack is an independent artist, songwriter, and studio operator based in Denver, North Carolina. Through his work with Andrejack and his recording practice at PMA Recording Studios, he creates original music rooted in craft, collaboration, and long-term artistic development. Andrejack's album Hope serves as the artistic foundation for his next phase of work and for the broader Hope Sessions initiative.

## Artistic Practice Overview

My work centers on original songwriting, recording, and production, with a focus on building music that is emotionally direct, professionally executed, and durable beyond a single release cycle. Through Andrejack, I have developed an artistic practice that combines songwriting, arrangement, studio craft, and a commitment to making music that connects with listeners on both a personal and public level. My previous work, including the album Hope, established a clear artistic direction that I now want to deepen through a more focused and intentional recording process.

## Project Narrative

### Project Description

Hope Sessions: New Work Recording Project is a three-song recording and finishing cycle that extends the themes and artistic direction introduced by Andrejack's album Hope. The project will support the writing, arranging, recording, editing, and completion of three new original songs that represent the next chapter of my creative practice.

The work will be developed in a professional recording environment at PMA Recording Studios, where I will be able to refine arrangements, capture strong performances, and bring each song to a finished state. The project is designed not as a general studio operation request but as a clearly defined artistic-development project focused on the creation and completion of new work.

The finished songs will provide both release-ready material and a stronger portfolio of work samples for future opportunities, collaborations, and public-facing initiatives under the broader Hope Sessions concept.

### Why This Project Matters Now

This project matters now because it gives me the time, structure, and support to move from a strong existing artistic foundation into a more mature and intentional next body of work. Hope was an important creative marker, but this project is about what comes next: expanding my songwriting, strengthening production decisions, refining performance capture, and building a more cohesive artistic trajectory.

For an independent artist working outside a major market center, access to a disciplined recording process and the ability to complete work at a high level are essential to growth. This project gives me a realistic and meaningful way to advance my practice without overreaching beyond what a first-cycle direct artist grant should support.

### How The Project Advances My Creative Practice

This project advances my creative practice in three ways. First, it deepens my songwriting and arrangement process by requiring me to shape a coherent, high-quality cycle of new work rather than isolated tracks. Second, it strengthens my production and finishing discipline by taking the songs through a complete process from development to release readiness. Third, it helps define a clearer long-term artistic identity around Hope Sessions, which can support future collaborations, performances, and community-facing formats.

In five-year terms, this project is an important bridge between previous work and a more developed body of music that can support broader artistic visibility, stronger portfolio materials, and more ambitious future projects.

### Feasibility

The project is intentionally scoped to remain achievable within the grant period and within a modest artist-support budget. Limiting the project to three songs makes it possible to maintain quality while keeping the timeline and expenses realistic. The workflow is straightforward: pre-production and writing refinement, tracking, overdubs and editing, then final finishing through mixing and mastering support.

Because the project is rooted in an existing artistic practice and supported by an established working studio environment, it is practical to complete without overpromising. The budget can remain tightly aligned to the narrative by focusing on direct project costs such as collaborator support, specialist finishing, and related session expenses.

## Evaluation-Criteria Alignment

### Demonstration Of Vibrant Vocational Creative Practice

This application is grounded in an ongoing artistic practice that includes original songwriting, recording, production, and previous released work through Andrejack. The album Hope demonstrates continuity, seriousness, and an existing body of work from which this new project grows.

### Feasibility Of The Proposed Project

The project is narrow by design: three songs, a defined workflow, and a budget centered on direct completion costs rather than generalized operations. This keeps the project realistic and internally consistent.

### Contribution To The Advancement Of The Applicant's Creative Practice

The proposal directly supports the next stage of artistic growth by deepening songwriting, production, finishing, and portfolio quality. It creates a stronger artistic bridge from prior work to future public and collaborative opportunities.

## Budget Narrative

The project budget should focus on expenses that are essential to completing the three-song recording cycle at a strong professional level. These may include collaborator fees, outside musician support, mixing and mastering costs, session-specific materials, and limited release-readiness expenses if the local guidelines support them.

The project should not be framed around general studio overhead or business-improvement costs. PMA Recording Studios should be described as the creative infrastructure that makes the work possible, not as the funded objective.

For actual line-building, use the companion worksheet in the budget document rather than improvising figures inside the portal.

## Working Timeline

1. Pre-production, final writing decisions, and arrangement refinement.
2. Tracking sessions for the three selected songs.
3. Overdubs, editing, and performance refinement.
4. Mixing and mastering.
5. Release-readiness preparation and work-sample documentation.
