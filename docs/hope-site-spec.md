# Hope Site Specification

## Product Goal

Create a modern single-page HTML5/CSS experience that gives Hope Sessions a credible public home and supports outreach, exposure, and trust without waiting for the full PMA rebuild.

## Primary Users

1. arts and community partners
2. potential funders or reviewers checking public credibility
3. artists interested in the work or future participation
4. general visitors trying to understand Paul Andrejack, PMA, and Hope Sessions

## Primary Conversion Goals

1. establish credibility immediately
2. explain the initiative in under one minute
3. generate partner and artist inquiries
4. serve as a clean reference point in grant and outreach conversations

## Page Structure

### Section 1: Hero

Content:

- Hope Sessions headline
- one-sentence mission
- subheading linking Paul Andrejack, PMA, and the idea of Hope
- primary CTA for partnership or inquiry
- secondary CTA to explore the story or hear the work

Goal:

- communicate what this is without forcing visitors to infer the purpose

### Section 2: Why Hope

Content:

- short story connecting Andrejack's album Hope to the new initiative
- statement that this is a real artistic foundation, not a manufactured brand theme

Goal:

- establish authenticity and emotional coherence

### Section 3: About Paul Andrejack And PMA

Content:

- concise founder bio
- selected career proof points
- studio role and creative capabilities
- selected artist or industry credibility markers

Goal:

- answer why this person and this studio are credible stewards of the initiative

### Section 4: What Hope Sessions Includes

Content:

- first-cycle project description
- future-facing formats such as access days, workshops, or stories of hope
- short plain-language explanation of who benefits and how

Goal:

- translate the initiative into concrete offerings rather than mood or aspiration

### Section 5: Community Need And Public Value

Content:

- concise explanation of the Lincoln County and regional artist-access gap
- statement of how professional recording infrastructure can serve creation and development

Goal:

- support grant and partner credibility without overclaiming impact

### Section 6: Proof

Content:

- work sample embeds or links
- selected credits or testimonials
- project or studio photos
- optional media mentions or quotes

Goal:

- make the page evidentiary, not just descriptive

### Section 7: Partner Pathways

Content:

- schools
- churches
- nonprofits
- arts organizations
- what collaboration could look like

Goal:

- make outreach actionable for institutions

### Section 8: Inquiry And Contact

Content:

- contact form or strong CTA
- email, phone, and location context
- inquiry categories such as partner, artist, studio, or media

Goal:

- convert interest into structured next steps

## Content Requirements

### Required Before Launch

- final headline and one-sentence positioning
- concise Paul Andrejack bio
- approved Hope Sessions initiative description
- at least two strong proof elements
- one partner-facing CTA
- one artist-facing CTA

### Strongly Recommended

- project photography or studio imagery
- one to three embedded tracks or media samples
- two to four short testimonial or credibility snippets
- a simple FAQ section

## Design Direction

The page should feel musically serious, contemporary, and warm rather than corporate or nonprofit-generic. The visual system should support the theme of Hope without becoming sentimental or soft-focus.

Use:

- bold typography
- strong contrast
- editorial spacing
- image-led sections with disciplined copy density
- clear section rhythm and hierarchy

Avoid:

- stock nonprofit aesthetics
- vague inspirational slogans without evidence
- generic agency-style web copy
- burying the CTA under too much story

## Technical Requirements

1. mobile-first responsive layout
2. strong Core Web Vitals baseline
3. semantic HTML5 structure
4. accessible headings, forms, and color contrast
5. clear metadata and social preview setup
6. reusable component logic if later folded into a broader PMA rebuild

## Accessibility Requirements

1. full keyboard navigation
2. meaningful heading hierarchy
3. accessible form labels and validation messages
4. alt text for meaningful images
5. sufficient contrast and reduced-motion support

## Launch Readiness Standard

Do not launch if the page is visually attractive but still weak on proof. The minimum acceptable launch has to combine narrative clarity, at least some evidence, a real CTA path, and a modern accessible experience.
