# PMA Web Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap defines a separate but connected web modernization workstream for PMA Recording Studios. The goal is not just a prettier website. The goal is to improve public outreach, credibility, partner confidence, and grant-adjacent trust signals while supporting the Hope initiative as it develops.

## Why This Matters

The current public site already contains useful credibility assets:

- Paul Andrejack's identity as owner, engineer, producer, and artist
- Existing PMA service offerings
- Social links and contact information
- Client and industry-name credibility

But the site also appears structurally dated and thin for 2026 expectations. Based on the publicly accessible pages reviewed, the current experience is largely brochure-style and service-led, with limited narrative packaging for:

- artistic identity
- project credibility
- initiative storytelling
- community-facing programming
- partnership outreach
- modern lead capture and trust-building

That creates a gap. The grant strategy is becoming clearer and stronger, but the public web surface does not yet reflect that evolution.

## Recommended Web Strategy

Do this in two layers, not one.

### Layer 1: Fast Supplemental Page

Build a single modern HTML5/CSS page or microsite section tied to the existing PMA site. This is the lowest-friction way to establish a public-facing home for the Hope initiative without waiting for a full site rebuild.

This page should do five jobs:

1. Explain who Paul Andrejack is and why the work is credible.
2. Introduce Hope Sessions as an artist-centered and community-facing initiative.
3. Show that the studio is infrastructure for music creation, access, and future programming.
4. Create a professional surface for partner outreach, community visibility, and future grant references.
5. Capture inquiry interest from artists, partners, schools, churches, and nonprofits.

### Layer 2: Full PMA 2026 Refresh

After the supplemental page proves the messaging, redesign the broader PMA site so the main brand and the Hope initiative work together instead of feeling like separate worlds.

The upgraded site should preserve the commercial studio offering while making room for:

- artist development positioning
- Hope initiative storytelling
- community access and workshop pathways
- partner-facing credibility
- stronger media, work samples, and testimonials
- more modern mobile, accessibility, and contact UX

## Site Findings From Current Public Pages

The current site presents:

- About page with PMA studio description, credibility references, and Paul Andrejack bio
- Services page with recording, mixing, mastering, and other services pricing
- Contact page with inquiry form and contact details

The current site does not yet appear to strongly present:

- a contemporary homepage narrative
- a clear initiative or mission layer
- a dedicated artist-development or community-access story
- a refined portfolio or case-study structure
- a modern conversion path for different audience types
- a 2026-standard visual system or editorial hierarchy

## Best Near-Term Information Architecture

If the first step is a supplemental page, the structure should be:

1. Hero: Hope Sessions headline, one-sentence mission, clear visual identity, one primary call to action.
2. Credibility: Paul Andrejack bio, Andrejack and Hope context, PMA experience, selected proof points.
3. Why It Exists: concise statement of local artist-access need and why PMA is building this initiative.
4. What It Includes: recording project, artist development, future access sessions, workshops, and partnership potential.
5. For Partners: a short section for schools, churches, nonprofits, and arts organizations.
6. For Artists: what kind of future participation or inquiry is welcome.
7. Proof: media, work samples, photos, testimonials, past artist work, or selected credits.
8. Contact: dedicated inquiry form or tailored contact CTA.

## Messaging Rules

The page should feel artist-led, credible, and current.

Use:

- Paul Andrejack as a real artist and studio founder
- Hope as the initiative's artistic and public-facing theme
- the studio as enabling infrastructure
- concrete language about music creation, access, development, and community-facing growth

Avoid:

- vague inspiration language with no project specifics
- "fund my studio" positioning
- overclaiming impact before programs are operating
- mixing commercial studio copy and grant language in the same hero without separation

## Recommended 2026 Build Phases

### Phase 0: Positioning And Discovery

Define audience segments and decide whether the supplemental page lives as:

- a standalone page on the current domain
- a Hope initiative subpage inside the PMA site
- a temporary microsite that later merges into a full redesign

Deliverables:

- page goal
- audience map
- messaging hierarchy
- content inventory
- proof inventory

### Phase 1: Supplemental Launch

Ship the one-page modern public surface first.

Deliverables:

- page copy
- design direction
- responsive HTML/CSS build
- mobile-first QA
- analytics and lead capture
- partner inquiry CTA

### Phase 2: Credibility Expansion

Add the supporting layers that make the page believable.

Deliverables:

- artist bio block
- Hope project story
- selected credits and testimonials
- media embeds or work sample gallery
- photography and visual refresh

### Phase 3: Full PMA Rebuild

Redesign the core PMA site around a modern structure.

Deliverables:

- new homepage
- refined services pages
- portfolio or selected-work section
- initiative/program pages
- improved contact conversion flow
- accessibility and performance pass

## Agent Team For This Workstream

### Control

- Agents Orchestrator
- Senior Project Manager
- Project Shepherd

### Strategy And Structure

- Product Manager: define audience and conversion goals
- UX Researcher: assess current clarity gaps and audience needs
- UX Architect: define information architecture and page logic
- Brand Guardian: keep PMA and Hope aligned without muddled positioning

### Design And Frontend

- UI Designer: establish a modern 2026 visual direction
- Visual Storyteller: shape the emotional and editorial flow of the Hope page
- Frontend Developer: implement the HTML5/CSS page or site refresh
- Accessibility Auditor: make sure the new experience is usable, not just attractive

### Narrative And Outreach

- Technical Writer: write clear, usable page copy
- Content Creator: shape artist-facing and partner-facing messaging
- Developer Advocate: help translate credibility and craft into compelling public narrative
- Social Media Strategist: align the page with amplification and public rollout

### QA And Evidence

- Reality Checker: prevent unsupported claims or empty branding
- Evidence Collector: ensure all proof points, credits, and claims are substantiated
- Workflow Optimizer: connect site updates to outreach and intake flow

## Sequencing Relative To Grants

This web workstream should support the grant strategy, not delay it.

Recommended order:

1. Finalize the Hope initiative concept and first application narrative.
2. Launch a simple but strong supplemental Hope page.
3. Use that page for outreach, partner conversations, and credibility.
4. Fold the learning from that page into a full PMA redesign later.

## Best Recommendation

Do not wait for a full PMA rebuild before publishing the stronger narrative. The right move is to create a supplemental Hope Sessions one-page experience first, then let that become the editorial and design seed for the broader 2026 PMA site refresh.
