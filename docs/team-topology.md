# Full-Team Topology

## Team Doctrine

This project uses every available agent in `agency-agents` as part of a single mission team. The team is not flat. It is structured so the full roster is available without turning orchestration into noise.

## Activation Layers

### Layer 1: Control Plane

- Agents Orchestrator controls sequencing, handoffs, retries, and quality gates.
- Senior Project Manager maintains task structure.
- Project Shepherd manages execution rhythm.

### Layer 2: Core Grant Team

These divisions are active on nearly every cycle:

- Product
- Project management
- Support
- Sales
- Marketing
- Design

### Layer 3: Specialist Bench

These divisions activate when their expertise creates a concrete advantage:

- Academic for research depth, narrative rigor, and cultural framing
- Finance for budgets, ROI logic, and funding models
- Developer relations for public narrative and documentation quality
- Testing for red-team review and evidence standards
- Specialized for orchestration, compliance, workflow design, identity, and stakeholder process

### Layer 4: Reserve Bench

These divisions are kept available for unusual grant angles, adjacent partnerships, or future expansion:

- Engineering
- Data analytics
- Cybersecurity
- Spatial computing
- Manufacturing
- Game development

They are not excluded. They are simply non-primary unless the opportunity requires them. Examples:

- Engineering can package a technical portal, CRM, or intake workflow.
- Data analytics can build funder reporting and impact dashboards.
- Cybersecurity can support data-handling requirements for youth programs.
- Game development and spatial agents can support immersive arts, interactive exhibits, or creative-tech grants.

## Division Responsibilities

### Product

Convert the vague goal of "get grants" into a prioritized portfolio of opportunities, narratives, and deadlines.

### Project Management

Maintain the operating cadence, submission calendar, and accountability model.

### Support

Own compliance, reporting, executive summaries, finance tracking, and post-submission operations.

### Sales

Handle partner mapping, sponsor outreach, proposal strategy, and relationship conversion.

### Marketing

Build the outward-facing narrative around the studio's mission, community value, and artist impact.

### Design

Package the story into strong one-sheets, decks, application assets, and brand-consistent materials.

### Testing

Act as the grant red team. Evidence Collector, Reality Checker, and Workflow Optimizer are especially important.

## Required Handoffs

1. Discovery to strategy: opportunity map, eligibility assumptions, and local ecosystem notes.
2. Strategy to foundation: chosen narratives, target applications, budget assumptions, and proof requirements.
3. Foundation to build: draft-ready assets and missing-document list.
4. Build to harden: final draft package, partner list, and unresolved risks.
5. Harden to submit: GO decision, attachments checklist, and submission instructions.

## Practical Rule

The generated inventory is the authoritative answer to "who is on the team?" The phase plans are the authoritative answer to "who should be active right now?"
