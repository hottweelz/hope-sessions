# Hope Sessions — Services

Hope Sessions provides professional recording and artist development services through PMA Recording Studios in Denver, NC. Services are available to individual artists and community organizations in Lincoln County and the greater Denver, NC area.

## Recording Studio Sessions

Full-service session recording at PMA Recording Studios. The studio operates a Tascam DM-3200 and Mackie consoles alongside outboard gear from Focusrite, Avalon, Lexicon, TC Electronic, Slate, Waves, Pultec, and Fairchild. Analog and digital signal chains. Experienced engineer on every session.

**Who this serves**: Independent artists at any skill level, from first-time studio visitors to experienced performers seeking a professional room.

## Artist Development

Hands-on production and development guidance from Paul Andrejack, who has 20+ years of recording, mixing, mastering, and live sound production experience. Development is tailored to each artist — from signal chain basics and scratch tracks to professional arrangement feedback and production decisions.

**Who this serves**: Songwriters and performers who need experienced guidance to take rough ideas to finished, release-ready recordings.

## Mixing and Mastering

Professional mixing and mastering for independently recorded or session-recorded material. Delivered to release-ready standards across all major streaming platforms.

## Community and Education Programming

Workshop series, after-school music production programs, mentorship sessions, and story-centered music projects for schools, nonprofits, faith communities, and youth organizations in Lincoln County. Programming is designed to fit the actual people in the room — no prior experience required.

**Who this serves**: Schools, churches, nonprofits, arts organizations, and community groups seeking a professional creative partner invested in Lincoln County.

## Grant-Supported Access

Selected artists participate at reduced or no cost through grant funding. The Hope Sessions initiative is supported in part through an Artist Support Grant application to the Arts & Science Council Charlotte-Mecklenburg consortium for Lincoln County (September 2026 deadline). Grant support removes the financial barrier between independent artists and professional-quality recording.
