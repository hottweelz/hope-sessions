# The Hope Sessions Initiative

Hope Sessions is a recording initiative led by Paul Andrejack at PMA Recording Studios in Denver, North Carolina. It is structured around six interrelated phases, beginning with original music creation and building toward permanent community access to professional recording infrastructure in Lincoln County.

## 1. New Work Recording

New original songs extending the world of the album *Hope* by Andrejack — tracked with discipline, finished to a real standard, released as completed work. Not demos shelved for later. The sessions opening Hope Sessions as a formal initiative are focused recording projects at PMA Recording Studios in Denver, NC: new songwriting by Paul Andrejack, production decisions made with the same discipline the album *Hope* was built with, and finished recordings ready for independent release.

## 2. All-Level Artist Development

Working with artists wherever they are — first studio visit or tenth album — to develop rough ideas into finished, release-ready recordings. No assumed baseline. No shortcuts on the result. Development at Hope Sessions is tailored to each artist: signal chain guidance and scratch tracks for first-timers; professional production and engineering judgment for experienced performers. Every artist leaves with a completed recording, not a rough draft.

## 3. Removing Barriers to Entry

Professional studio time and experienced guidance cost several hundred dollars per session — before mixing, mastering, or production costs. Most independent artists in Lincoln County cannot afford this. Grant funding removes that financial wall, covering session time, equipment, engineering, and production costs so that participation is determined by the strength of the idea, not the size of the budget.

## 4. Community Creative Infrastructure

A long-term model for connecting professional recording support to schools, nonprofits, and community organizations in Lincoln County. After-school music production programs, workshop series, mentorship sessions with schools and faith communities, and story-centered music projects. The professional studio, experience, and relationships already exist here. This phase formalizes the community access model that converts those existing private resources into a public creative asset.

## 5. Why Grants Fund This Work

Arts grants exist to close the gap between what independent artists can pay and what professional-quality recording actually costs. Hope Sessions is a direct match for that purpose. The initiative is applying for an Artist Support Grant through the Arts & Science Council Charlotte-Mecklenburg consortium for Lincoln County, with a September 2026 submission deadline.

## 6. Investing in Lincoln County Youth

Most young people in Lincoln County have never been inside a professional recording studio or had the chance to make original music in a real room with professional equipment. Hope Sessions is committed to programming that puts professional studio access, mentorship, and musical education directly in front of local youth — workshops, sessions, and educational collaborations with Lincoln County schools and community organizations.
