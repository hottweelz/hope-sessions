## Extended Description (150 words)

Hope Sessions is a recording initiative led by Paul Andrejack at PMA Recording Studios in Denver, North Carolina. Named after the album *Hope* by the indie rock band Andrejack — a distinct musical entity fronted by Paul Andrejack — the initiative begins with focused new original music sessions and grows toward broader community impact: professional studio access, artist development, mentorship, and musical education for artists and youth in Lincoln County who currently have no path to professional recording infrastructure.

Paul Andrejack brings 20+ years of recording, production, engineering, and live sound experience. He is an Internet Records recording artist, has co-written with Grammy Award winners Mark Mangold and Mark Gatz, collaborated with The Alessi Brothers, and consulted for Bakithi Kumalo (bassist for Paul Simon) and Kent Marcum (composer for Harpo Productions). The initiative is supported in part through an Artist Support Grant application to the Arts & Science Council Charlotte-Mecklenburg consortium for Lincoln County, with a September 2026 submission deadline.

## What Hope Sessions Is Not

- **Not** a free recording studio — grant funding supports selected artists; access is not unlimited or automatic
- **Not** a replacement for music schools or formal education — it's a working professional studio run by a working professional
- **Not** a startup or proposal — PMA Recording Studios is an active, operational studio; Hope Sessions formalizes the community access model
- **Not** located in Charlotte or Raleigh — it is specifically in Denver, NC, Lincoln County, which is underserved relative to major NC arts markets
