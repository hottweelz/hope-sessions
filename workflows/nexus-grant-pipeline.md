# NEXUS Grant Pipeline

This adapts the `agency-agents` NEXUS operating model to a grant-acquisition workflow for a Denver, NC recording studio.

## Control Principle

All agents discovered in `generated/agent-inventory.json` are members of the team. The Orchestrator activates them by phase and relevance. The goal is comprehensive coverage without wasting cycles on simultaneous noise.

## Phase 0: Discovery

### Objective

Validate opportunity landscape, eligibility, local funder pathways, and public-benefit positioning.

### Primary Agents

- Agents Orchestrator
- Trend Researcher
- UX Researcher
- Feedback Synthesizer
- Analytics Reporter
- Legal Compliance Checker
- Finance Tracker
- Tool Evaluator
- Government Digital Presales Consultant

### Outputs

1. Funding landscape report
2. Eligibility matrix
3. Denver and Lincoln County arts ecosystem map
4. Fit-ranked opportunity list

## Full-Team Rule

Every available agent is considered part of the team, but each phase has an active core, an advisory bench, and a reserve bench.

1. Active core agents produce direct deliverables.
2. Advisory agents are consulted when a blocker or expertise gap appears.
3. Reserve agents remain available for edge cases, messaging, outreach, compliance, or packaging needs.

## Quality Gate

No application is submitted until all of the following are true:

1. Eligibility is verified.
2. Budget is internally consistent.
3. Narrative does not pitch the studio as a business subsidy request.
4. Community impact claims are backed by a realistic delivery plan.
5. Required attachments are complete.
6. A Reality Checker review returns a GO decision.
